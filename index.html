Research


How is searching for information in an online health community the same as searching the rest of the Web?  Of course, it is not at all the same! At State University of New York at Plattsburgh, we are confident there is a better way to bring you together with the community resources you need.

Online discussion communities are becoming increasingly popular among web users, where an extensive amount of discussion and commenting takes place. However, it is diﬃcult to search these conversations as search engines are not optimized for the conversation-structure of online communities. In this paper, we purpose a method of ranking search results based on the conversation-structure and using it to optimize a domain-speciﬁc search engine. To solve this problem, we design several extension to a general-purpose search engine, focusing on maintaining the conversation-structure throughout the extraction, calculating and ranking of search results. Results from our experiments show that users can ﬁnd optimized search results quicker as it is ranked higher than keyword based results.
